# Standardized Terminology

This document defines the standard terms and their usage for consistent academic writing within this repository.

## General Guidelines
- Use these terms consistently throughout all documents
- If you need to use a synonym, add it to this list with a cross-reference
- When introducing new terms, define them clearly and add them to the appropriate section

## Core Terms

### Research Methodology
- **Empirical Study**: Research based on observed and measured phenomena
- **Qualitative Research**: Research focusing on understanding concepts, thoughts, and experiences
- **Quantitative Research**: Research that uses numerical data and statistical analysis
- **Mixed Methods**: Research combining qualitative and quantitative approaches
- **Sample**: The group of individuals participating in the study
- **Population**: The entire group about which information is desired

### Academic Writing
- **Thesis Statement**: A clear statement of the main argument or purpose of the paper
- **Literature Review**: A comprehensive summary of previous research on a topic
- **Methodology**: The systematic, theoretical analysis of the methods applied to a field of study
- **Findings**: The results of the research
- **Discussion**: Interpretation of the findings in the context of existing research

### Statistical Terms
- **Mean**: The average of a set of numbers
- **Median**: The middle value in a sorted list of numbers
- **Standard Deviation**: A measure of the amount of variation in a set of values
- **p-value**: The probability of obtaining results at least as extreme as the observed results
- **Correlation**: A statistical relationship between two variables
- **Causation**: A relationship where one event is the result of the occurrence of the other event

## Discipline-Specific Terms

### Psychology
- **Cognitive Dissonance**: The mental discomfort experienced when holding two conflicting beliefs
- **Operational Definition**: A statement of the procedures used to define research variables
- **Random Assignment**: Assigning participants to experimental and control groups by chance

### Business Studies
- **SWOT Analysis**: A strategic planning technique for identifying strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats
- **ROI (Return on Investment)**: A performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency of an investment
- **Stakeholder**: Any individual or group that can affect or is affected by an organization's actions

## Common Abbreviations
- **e.g.**: for example
- **i.e.**: that is
- **cf.**: compare
- **et al.**: and others (used in citations for works with multiple authors)
- **etc.**: and so forth
- **vs.**: versus

## Preferred Spellings
- email (not e-mail)
- internet (not Internet)
- website (not web site)
- online (not on-line)
- database (not data base)

## Terms to Avoid
- "Prove" (use "suggest," "indicate," or "support" instead)
- "A lot" (use "many," "much," or "a large number/amount of")
- "Very" (use more precise language)
- "Thing" (be specific about what you're referring to)
- "Get" (use more specific verbs like "obtain," "receive," "become")

## Formatting Conventions
- Write out numbers one through ten, use numerals for 11 and above
- Use the percent symbol (%) with numerals (e.g., 50%)
- Use the degree symbol (°) for temperatures and angles (e.g., 37°C, 90° angle)
- Use the multiplication symbol (×) for dimensions (e.g., 2 × 2 factorial design)
