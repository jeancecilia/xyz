# Academic Integrity Guidelines

## 1. Principles of Academic Integrity

### 1.1 Definition
Academic integrity means conducting research and scholarship in an honest, responsible, and ethical manner. It is fundamental to the academic community and ensures the credibility of academic work.

### 1.2 Core Values
- **Honesty**: Presenting work truthfully and accurately
- **Trust**: Maintaining mutual confidence in the research process
- **Fairness**: Giving proper credit to others' work
- **Respect**: Acknowledging the intellectual property of others
- **Responsibility**: Upholding ethical standards in research and writing

## 2. Plagiarism

### 2.1 Definition
Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's work, ideas, or words without proper attribution, presenting them as one's own.

### 2.2 Forms of Plagiarism
- **Verbatim plagiarism**: Copying text word-for-word without quotation marks or citation
- **Paraphrasing plagiarism**: Rewriting someone else's ideas without proper attribution
- **Self-plagiarism**: Reusing one's own previously published work without acknowledgment
- **Mosaic plagiarism**: Combining phrases from multiple sources without proper attribution
- **Incorrect citation**: Falsely attributing or misrepresenting sources

### 2.3 How to Avoid Plagiarism
- Always cite sources properly
- Use quotation marks for direct quotes
- Paraphrase correctly (changing both words and sentence structure)
- Keep detailed notes about your sources
- Use reference management software

## 3. Proper Citation Practices

### 3.1 When to Cite
- When quoting directly
- When paraphrasing someone else's ideas
- When using data, images, or other media created by others
- When building on others' work

### 3.2 Citation Styles
- Follow the APA/DGPs style as specified in `citation_style.md`
- Be consistent with your citation style throughout the document
- Include a complete reference list

## 4. Collaboration vs. Collusion

### 4.1 Permitted Collaboration
- Discussing course material with peers
- Forming study groups
- Seeking clarification from instructors or teaching assistants

### 4.2 Prohibited Collaboration (Collusion)
- Submitting work done by someone else as your own
- Working together on assignments unless explicitly allowed
- Sharing completed work with other students

## 5. Consequences of Academic Misconduct

### 5.1 Possible Sanctions
- Warning
- Reduction of grade
- Failing grade for the assignment
- Failing grade for the course
- Disciplinary action by the institution
- Revocation of academic degree (if discovered later)

### 5.2 Reporting Process
- Suspected violations should be reported to the course instructor
- The case will be reviewed according to institutional policies
- Students have the right to appeal decisions

## 6. Resources
- University writing center
- Reference management tools (e.g., Zotero, Mendeley)
- Plagiarism detection software
- Academic integrity office

## 7. Declaration of Academic Integrity
Include the following statement in your work:

```
I hereby declare that this work is my own and that I have not used any sources other than those indicated. All references to literature, electronic media, and other sources have been properly cited.

[Your Name]
[Date]
[Signature]
```
