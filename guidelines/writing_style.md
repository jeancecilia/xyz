# Writing Style Guidelines

## 1. General Principles
- Use clear, formal academic language
- Be precise and concise in your writing
- Maintain an objective tone
- Use gender-inclusive language
- Follow standard German orthography (Duden)

## 2. Language and Grammar
- Write in complete sentences
- Use active voice when possible
- Avoid colloquialisms and contractions
- Be consistent with terminology (see `terminology/default_terms.md`)

## 3. Formatting
- Use headings and subheadings to structure your text
- Use bullet points and numbered lists for better readability
- Highlight important terms in **bold** or *italics* when necessary
- Use blockquotes for longer quotations

## 4. Numbers and Units
- Spell out numbers one through twelve; use numerals for 13 and above
- Use the metric system (SI units)
- Include units of measurement after numbers (e.g., 10 cm, 25 kg)
- Use the decimal comma in German (e.g., 3,14 not 3.14)

## 5. Academic Integrity
- Always cite your sources properly
- Clearly distinguish between your ideas and those of others
- Avoid direct translations without proper attribution
- Be transparent about limitations in your work
